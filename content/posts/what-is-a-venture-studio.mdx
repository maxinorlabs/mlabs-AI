Over 90% of funded startups fail within five years. That number hasn't moved much in a decade — and it won't, as long as the dominant model stays the same.

The traditional VC model is simple: find a founder with a good idea, write a cheque, and hope the team figures it out. It works sometimes. But the failure rate tells the real story.

**Venture studios operate on a fundamentally different premise:** what if the risk of starting a company could be reduced before a single rupee of external capital is deployed?

## What Makes a Venture Studio Different

A venture studio doesn't wait for founders to walk through the door. It originates ideas internally, validates them with real market data, and then assembles the right team to execute — often a mix of in-house operators and external co-founders.

The result is a company that starts with:

- A validated problem statement
- An early distribution hypothesis
- Operational infrastructure already in place (legal, finance, design, tech)
- A network of investors who already trust the studio's thesis

This is what Maxinor calls **operator-led venture building** — we've lived inside these industries, we know where the inefficiencies are, and we build to solve them.

## The Numbers That Matter

Studios typically see **3–5x better survival rates** compared to traditionally funded startups at the seed stage. This isn't magic — it's the compounding effect of:

1. **Shared infrastructure** that eliminates duplicated setup costs across companies
2. **Pattern recognition** from running multiple builds simultaneously
3. **Fast, cheap experimentation** before committing to scale

## Why India, Why Now

India's startup ecosystem has matured enormously over the last decade. Capital is no longer the bottleneck — talent, market access, and operational know-how are. This is exactly where a studio model adds disproportionate value.

The next generation of category-defining companies won't all come from a 22-year-old with a laptop. Many will come from operators who deeply understand a market and have the infrastructure to build fast.

That's the bet Maxinor is making.

## FAQ

### What is a venture studio?

**A venture studio** is a company that builds startups from scratch using shared internal resources, operator expertise, and a validated thesis — rather than just providing capital to external founders. Studios typically co-found companies and hold equity from day one.

### How is a venture studio different from a VC fund?

A VC fund invests in companies that already exist. A venture studio creates companies — originating ideas, building teams, and providing operational support throughout the earliest and riskiest stage of company building.

### Are venture studios a good model for founders?

Yes — for the right founder. Studios offer infrastructure, networks, and co-building support in exchange for equity. Founders give up more ownership early, but get significantly de-risked execution support in return.

### What is operator-led venture building?

Operator-led venture building means the people building and backing the company have previously run businesses in the same sector. They bring direct execution experience — not just capital or advice.
